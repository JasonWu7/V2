Mental Health Disorders and Depression Data Statistics
Mental Health Disorders and Depression are critical aspects of the human experience that significantly impact individuals' lives, affecting their emotional and psychological well-being. These conditions encompass a wide range of mental health challenges, each with its unique characteristics and effects. Understanding these issues is essential to provide support, reduce stigma, and improve the overall mental health of individuals and communities.

The visualized data is sourced from Kaggle and presents data on patients with mental health issues from different countries from 1990 to 2017.

As of 2017, the global population affected by mental health issues reached a staggering 801,321,216 people, and mental health problems are prevalent in countries worldwide.

From the map, it is evident that countries like China, the United States, and India have significantly higher numbers of mental health issue patients compared to other countries.
(Note: The visualization data has been standardized, so the number of patients is not directly related to the total population of each country.)

Equally noteworthy is that Greenland has much lower numbers of mental health issue patients compared to almost all other countries.
The global number of individuals with mental disorders amounts to 10.6% of the world's total population, meaning that one in every ten people has experienced or is currently experiencing challenges related to mental health.



In extensive research within the field of mental health, a common phenomenon emerges: women are more prone to mental health issues than men. This phenomenon is also evident in our data.
This phenomenon is attributed to complex factors, such as biological elements, differences in the physiological makeup of males and females, the impact of hormonal fluctuations on emotions and mental health. For instance, psychological factors play a role, as women tend to be more emotionally oriented, and social factors, such as gender inequality in certain regions, also contribute to this trend.

Comparison
The chart on the left displays the top ten countries with the highest number of mental health issue patients, standardized for comparison. The value of top five countries are highlighted for additional emphasis. As mentioned earlier, countries such as China, India, the United States, and North America have the highest number of individuals with mental health disorders, with China leading by overwhelming numbers in almost all types of mental health issues. The chart allows us to compare that while these countries all have a significant number of patients, the difference in numbers among them is particularly substantial.

From an overall trend perspective, the data follows an exponential distribution. This implies that certain countries have significantly more mental health issue patients than others, and the majority of these patients may originate from specific regions. The reasons behind such significant disparities could be cultural differences, such as a lack of emphasis on mental health and education, or environmental factors, like high competition and stress in densely populated areas.
